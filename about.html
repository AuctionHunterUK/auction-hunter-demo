About AuctionSavvy

A private demonstration — a personalised morning briefing

EasyLive is the official place to discover, view and bid on auction lots. AuctionSavvy is designed to work alongside it, turning a dealer’s or collector’s standing interests into a clear, regularly updated shortlist.

Instead of repeatedly reviewing broad search results, users choose the types of items, locations and auction houses that matter to them. AuctionSavvy then presents relevant new lots in a simple, time-sensitive briefing—without attempting to replace EasyLive’s official catalogue or bidding service.

Designed for a ten-minute morning check

In around ten minutes, a dealer or collector should be able to see what is new within their chosen interests, what is local, what is selling soon and what may match either a customer request or a personal collecting priority—then follow any promising item directly to EasyLive for its official details and bidding.

Every planned feature follows the same principle: plain language, sensible defaults, minimal setup and only the controls that make finding and assessing items quicker.

EasyLive runs the auctions. AuctionSavvy helps busy dealers and collectors decide what deserves their attention.

This demonstration brings two complementary tools together.

Lots

A regularly updated, pre-filtered selection of pine furniture and butchers’ blocks appearing in UK auctions. The demonstration is already narrowed to these chosen interests, then organised into Local Auctions, UK-Wide Today and UK-Wide Later so that promising items can be assessed quickly by both location and urgency.

Every item links directly to its original EasyLive listing for official catalogue information and bidding.

Map

An interactive map of UK auction houses, colour-coded according to when their next sale takes place. A planned route-planning feature could help users discover auction houses along a journey and make collection planning easier.

Planned access and membership

Subject to an agreed EasyLive partnership and authorised data feed, AuctionSavvy could develop through a public preview, free map membership, personalised paid membership and an additional dealer sourcing service.

Morning Briefing — Member

A paid, personalised daily shortlist built from each member’s chosen interests, preferred auction houses and location.

Relevant lots would be organised by urgency and proximity, with new opportunities clearly highlighted. Members could adjust their interests and preferred local auction houses without having to manage a complicated set of controls.

Unlike the public Daily Showcase, members would control which types of items appear and receive a consistent briefing relevant to them.

Returning paid members would be taken directly to their Morning Briefing so that they could begin reviewing items immediately. Every lot would continue to link directly to EasyLive for official details and bidding.

Wanted — Dealer add-on

An additional sourcing service for dealers handling customer requests.

A dealer could describe an item naturally—for example, “a Victorian pine chest of drawers approximately 45 inches wide”—or paste only the relevant product requirements from a customer email. The service would retain the sourcing requirements rather than the original email or unnecessary customer details.

An authorised morning process could compare active requests with newly supplied catalogue information and identify possible matches using details such as item type, material, dimensions, location and sale date.

A match notification could include a photograph, catalogue dimensions, auction house, sale date, an explanation of why the item matched and a direct link to the official EasyLive listing.

These would be suggested matches rather than guarantees. Dealers would always be expected to verify the original catalogue information before advising a customer or bidding.

Map — Free member

Free registered access to the UK auction-house map, upcoming sale dates and route-planning tools.

After signing in, free members would be taken directly to the Map rather than being required to pass through a promotional landing page.

Daily Showcase — Public preview

A live, publicly accessible sample of the AuctionSavvy experience. Each day would feature a different automatically selected category—such as ceramics, pine furniture or artwork—using a limited selection of current lots.

Visitors could explore that day’s selection and follow items directly to EasyLive, but they could not choose the category, search the complete catalogue, set local preferences, view previous showcases or receive alerts.

The changing daily category would provide a genuine demonstration of AuctionSavvy without replacing the personalised and consistent service available to members.

Planned UK-wide delivery support

Finding a suitable item is only useful if collecting it is practical. AuctionSavvy would aim to introduce dealers and collectors to one or more independent specialist UK-wide delivery providers, helping them consider auctions beyond their usual collection area and widening the potential bidder reach of participating auction houses.

Initially, this could provide a simple route to request a delivery quotation. Subject to suitable agreements with delivery providers and the availability of authorised catalogue information, a later integration could use the auction-house location, the buyer’s delivery postcode, the item type and any available dimensions to provide a quick indicative quote alongside a lot.

By reducing delivery uncertainty before bidding, this could make more geographically distant purchases practical and bring additional qualified visitors to the official EasyLive listings.

This is a proposed capability rather than an existing delivery partnership. Any quotation, booking and service would be confirmed and provided by the independent delivery company.

Chrome companion and complementary tools

Alongside the main service, AuctionSavvy has an optional Chrome companion designed for people browsing EasyLive directly. In the current demonstration, hovering over an auction-house name reveals its address and postcode—information that can otherwise take several clicks to find.

This is a small example of AuctionSavvy’s wider principle: remove avoidable steps and make daily auction research quicker and clearer. Possible future additions could include auction-house shortcuts, private collection notes and enhanced multi-stop collection planning.

These would remain lightweight aids around the official EasyLive experience. Any broader integration affecting EasyLive’s website, listings or data would be developed transparently and in consultation with EasyLive.

Working responsibly with auction data

All lot and auction information displayed in this demonstration is sourced from EasyLive. Rights in the listings, descriptions and imagery remain with EasyLive, the participating auctioneers and their respective rights holders.

AuctionSavvy is currently a password-protected, invitation-only demonstration created to explore a potential partnership with EasyLive. It is not being offered as a commercial service, and every lot links directly back to EasyLive for viewing and bidding.

We recognise that EasyLive’s published site-usage terms require written permission for automated data collection. Any development beyond this demonstration would therefore be undertaken through an agreed data-access arrangement or partnership.

We will promptly remove any information or imagery at the request of EasyLive or the relevant rights holder.

Proposed data-use monitoring

A future partnership could also include an analytics-based monitoring capability designed to help protect catalogue information.

It could examine access patterns and flag unusual automated collection, bulk extraction or catalogue reuse for investigation.

This would be a proposed detection and alerting tool—not an automatic accusation or enforcement system—with its rules, thresholds and reporting agreed directly with EasyLive.
